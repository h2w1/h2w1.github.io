Abstract

Large Language Model (LLM) agents operating in multi-step environments are vulnerable to Cascading Errors, where a single initial hallucination leads to irreversible failure. Existing paradigms often struggle to balance reliability with computational efficiency, either relying on post-hoc corrections or expensive continuous oversight.

We propose the Confidence-Gated Player-Coach framework, a decision-theoretic approach to agent oversight. Our method (1) utilizes robust uncertainty signals to detect risk, (2) triggers the Coach only when the expected risk outweighs the intervention cost, and (3) enables Self-Evolution by distilling successful interventions back into the Player. This framework achieves superior performance and token efficiency across benchmarks like ALFWorld and WebShop without requiring additional training data.

Figure 1. The proposed framework. The Confidence Gate triggers external oversight only when high uncertainty is detected. Successful trajectories are then used to fine-tune the Player, creating a cycle of self-improvement.

Motivation & Limitations

While current agentic frameworks have made significant strides, they face critical trade-offs between accuracy and cost. We address these limitations directly:

  • vs. ReAct (Single-Agent Loop)
    Problem Biases reinforce themselves, leading to cascading errors.
    Our Solution External Oversight breaks the error loop via objective feedback.
  • vs. Reflexion (Post-hoc Correction)
    Problem Relies on trial-and-error (resetting after failure), resulting in wasted trials.
    Our Solution Real-time Intervention corrects errors before failure occurs within the episode.
  • vs. LAC (Continuous Critique)
    Problem High inefficiency due to always-on supervision.
    Our Solution Confidence Gating triggers the Coach only when necessary (Risk > Cost).

Methodology

1. Decision-Theoretic Gating

We frame intervention not as a heuristic, but as a Cost-Sensitive Decision Making problem. The Coach is invoked if and only if the risk of failure exceeds the cost of intervention.

$$g_t = \mathbb{I} \left[ \underbrace{\mathbb{E}[\mathcal{L}_{error}]}_{\text{Risk of Failure}} > \underbrace{C_{interv}}_{\text{Cost of Query}} \right]$$

2. Robust Confidence Signals

To ensure robustness across dynamic action spaces, we employ two normalized uncertainty metrics:

$$ \textbf{Global Uncertainty:} \quad \bar{H}_t = \frac{\log(\text{PPL})}{\log|\mathcal{A}_t|} $$
$$ \textbf{Local Ambiguity:} \quad m_t = \log P(a_{(1)}|x_t) - \log P(a_{(2)}|x_t) $$

3. Self-Evolution Loop

Successful trajectories‚Äîspecifically those guided by the Coach‚Äîserve as high-quality demonstrations. By fine-tuning the Player on this data, the agent internalizes the Coach's reasoning capabilities, progressively evolving from assisted reasoning to full autonomy.
